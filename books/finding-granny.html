Finding Granny

By Andrew Abel  |  Illustrated by Graeme Holding

A rhyming picture book about children searching for their grandmother using visual clues hidden throughout the house — until they find her for a surprise hug. Inspired by the author's father's hand-drawn treasure hunts, the story teaches readers how to play the game themselves. The simple vocabulary and visual clues work for both readers and pre-readers alike.

★★★★★
"Finding Granny by Andrew Abel is a delightful illustrated children's book. The story follows two children seeking out their granny's hiding place, using clues left along the way. The story details the game so that readers can play it too. This fun way to play with the children is an interactive and inclusive way to include even the youngest readers. The illustrations pair wonderfully with the hunt for clues and offer bright colorful glimpses of the children as they play their game. Graeme Holding does a remarkable job of bringing this story to life with his talented illustrations. The story is written in a rhyming style, and the story and images are sure to please young listeners as they hear the story read and look at the amusing pictures as they follow along."

About the Illustrator

Graeme Holding is originally from Helsby, Cheshire, and now lives in Runcorn with his wife and four children. His illustration style is bright, fun, traditional but quirky. He completed a Children's Book Illustration course at the London Art College. Portfolio: graemeholdingillustration.com
